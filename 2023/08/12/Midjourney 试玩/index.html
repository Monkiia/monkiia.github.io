Midjourney 试玩

很早就想尝试AI 绘画, 主要是自己的40系显卡蠢蠢欲动 请求出战

打开Bilibili 学习， 看到Stable Difussion 几十小时的视频瞬间就不想学了。。

我一直觉得吧，做什么事情都得是现有motivation/intuition，然后再深入学习。所以决定自己先不炼丹，先用用市场上最火的工具Midjourney！

Midjourney作为一款AI绘画软件，有几点非常有意思

  1. Midjourney的基本上是没有前端的，你要使用它得从discord上的聊天室里输入prompt来生成图片 我认为这是非常正确的选择，Midjourney作为一个只有十几个人的小作坊，他没有钱也没有能力去搭建一个专门的前端网站（像ChatGPT一样）， 搭建前端网站势必就要选择CDN，设计登录/AuthN, 还要选择靠谱的防火墙去防范黑客攻击，然后单独的用户管理还要考虑到如何防止账号共享，这样势必就会有更多的逻辑和资源牵连其中，它家自然是没有这个资源的。将这一切问题让渡给discord平台就不用再考虑这么多问题。再换句话说，等Midjourney完成了那种前端网站，市场肯定就已经被抢占完了，创业公司的逻辑永远是move fast
  2. Midjourney 作为AI prompt，需要非常精准的prompt去生成你想要的图片，就像我上图中打的An apple a day keeps doctor away 基本上就是个垃圾prompt，生成出来的图片没什么用。更加精准一点的我打了一个promot, a man with sword stand lonely facing the moon and world 就精准很多，它可以非常好看的打出我想要的图片

  3. 你可以根据已经生成的图片或者自己提供的图片，去一步步完善成你想要的图片，基本上就是使用一些command prompt去让他更合你心意，这个比较高阶，我也没有继续玩下去，打算有时间去看点视频教程

总结，AI这波热度直接让很多AI工具变得超级火，我不认为这是一个昙花一现的事情，它确确实实的提升了人们的生产力，从写代码，写文章，画图，创作，方方面面的让人们变得更加专业。我看到很多专业的绘画师开始使用这样的工具去快速构建这样的prototype，专业的程序员使用Github Copilot 或者是 AWS CodeWhisperer 这样的工具去提升生产力。它们毫无疑问使用帮助的，但是专业的绘画需要大局观，需要微调。专业的程序员需要好的系统设计和在工业级项目里钻研出来的经验。这些可能目前的AI工具还无法提供。乐观来讲，人类的生产力再往前所未有的方向发展，人人都可以快速的创造点什么出来。悲观的来讲，算力与计算资源依旧是被大平台们垄断，这样的快速前进可能它们才是真正的赢家。

I’ve wanted to try AI painting for a long time, mainly because my 40 series graphics card has been itching to be put to use. Requesting permission to deploy it!

Opened up Bilibili to learn about Stable Diffusion, but the prospect of spending dozens of hours on a single video quickly killed my motivation.

I’ve always believed that you need initial motivation/intuition for anything you do, and then you can dive deep into learning. So, I’ve decided not to start from scratch myself, but instead use the trendiest tool on the market right now – Midjourney!

Midjourney, as an AI painting software, has several interesting aspects:

  1. Midjourney is essentially frontend-less. To use it, you have to input prompts in a chat room on Discord to generate images. I believe this is a very smart choice. With only a small team of around a dozen people, Midjourney lacks the resources to build a dedicated frontend website (like ChatGPT). Setting up a frontend involves using CDNs, designing login/authentication systems, selecting reliable firewalls to prevent hacks, and managing user accounts to prevent sharing, all of which require additional resources and complexity. Handing off these problems to the Discord platform eliminates many of these concerns. In other words, if Midjourney were to create a frontend website, the market would likely already be saturated by the time it’s completed, as the logic of startup companies is always to move fast.

  2. As an AI prompt, Midjourney requires highly accurate prompts to generate the desired images. For instance, the prompt “An apple a day keeps the doctor away” that I used in the image above is essentially a poor prompt and the resulting image is not useful. A more precise prompt, such as “a man with a sword stands lonely facing the moon and the world,” produces images that are much closer to what I’m looking for.


  1. You can refine the generated images step by step using existing or user-provided images, employing various command prompts to tailor them to your liking. This is a more advanced feature that I haven’t explored further, but I plan to watch some tutorial videos when I have the time.

To sum up, the AI trend has propelled many AI tools into the limelight. I don’t think this is a fleeting phenomenon; it genuinely enhances people’s productivity. From coding and writing articles to drawing and creating, these tools have elevated various aspects of human output. I’ve observed many professional artists using tools like these to quickly prototype their work, and expert programmers employing tools like GitHub Copilot or AWS CodeWhisperer to boost their productivity. These tools are undoubtedly helpful, but professional artistry requires a holistic perspective and fine-tuning. Proficient programmers need solid system design skills and experience gained from industrial-level projects, which these current AI tools might not yet provide. Optimistically, human productivity is evolving in unprecedented ways, allowing everyone to rapidly create valuable content. Pessimistically, computational power and resources remain monopolized by major platforms, making them potentially the true winners in this rapid advancement.
