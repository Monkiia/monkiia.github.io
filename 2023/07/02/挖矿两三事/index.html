挖矿两三事

发表于2023-07-02|更新于2023-07-02

挖矿两三事

我最初接触挖矿是因为学习深度学习课程。那是2020年冬天，我的笔记本电脑旧旧的，没有独立显卡。老师推荐我们使用Google的Colab来训练参数，而对于更大的模型，我们使用学校的HPC集群。HPC集群经常被使用，特别是作业截止日期前的几天。我觉得很困扰，但班里有几个同学有独立的显卡3070，他们可以用来训练模型。我真的非常羡慕。

然而，当我上网搜索时，发现根本买不到3070，因为当时比特币和以太坊经历了一波大行情，价格涨了好几倍。矿工们赚得盆满钵满（一个显卡一天可以挖100块钱，3070的原价大概是3000左右。这意味着只需要三个月就能回本）。结果，3070的价格在短时间内猛涨到了一万块人民币（是的，翻了两三倍）……所以我根本也买不到显卡。

然而，2021年3月，一线希望出现了。那时候，唯一有机会买到显卡的方法就是购买整机，像惠普或戴尔的整机里面有3060Ti或3070。一台整机的价格大约是1699/1799美元。于是，我果断买了三台整机，在佛罗里达州奥兰多的Airbnb房间里愉快地挖矿了三个月。我使用的是NiceHash，当时也没有想过组装矿机，因为我对电脑组装并不是很熟悉。我曾经考虑去抢Best Buy的原价卡，但是前一天下午五点，就会有很多美国人在街上搭帐篷排队等卡，我根本无法竞争，早上去的时候已经没有名额了。

我在9月份去了纽约，一算账，发现通过整机挖矿实际上是很亏的。我根本不需要那些CPU、内存和主板，最好的方法是自己组装矿机，争取抢到原价的显卡。纽约的Micro Center是可以买到显卡的，但你得每天盯着它们。于是，我写了一个脚本，部署在AWS Lambda上，然后使用EventBridge定时检查，一旦有货就通过SNS发送到我的手机短信上。就是靠这样，我成功抢到了7、8张原价卡。我并不挑剔，主要选择的是3060Ti、3070、6700XT等。再加上在eBay上买的卡，到2021年底，我手上总共有28张GPU。我组装了三台矿机，开心地挖矿。纽约的冬天很冷，但我家里很温暖。

当时挖矿已经变得非常火热，以太坊社区开始将升级到2.0（Shanghai Forge）作为重要路线图。很多人开始担心挖矿可能很快就无法继续了，所以有人开始卖掉手上的矿卡。我对此并不担心，因为我并不相信ETH 2.0的升级。在我看来，唯一真正去中心化共识的方法是分布式哈希，而2.0版本所采用的几十个服务器本质上还是中心化的方法，完全背离了区块链的初衷。因此，即使以太坊不能再进行挖矿，我相信还是可以挖其他币的。

我挖矿的收益实际上非常不错，最高时挖出的币价值大约是两万美元。这实际上覆盖了很多矿卡的成本，至于电费，我把它看作是取暖费用，所以并不在意。

真正的转折发生在2022年春天，当时美联储开始缩减资产负债表，利率飙升，导致加密货币市场血洗，价格直接跌去三分之一。那时，我被打击得体无完肤，毕竟看着自己的币价从两万美元跌到6000美元，这是大多数人都难以接受的。很多人开始卖卡，而电费仍然很昂贵，每月接近1000美元。继续挖下去就会亏本了。同时，我也即将毕业，所以我收拾好所有的矿卡，带着它们来到西雅图开始新的生活。

回首当初的挖矿经历，感觉就像是一场梦境。你感觉财富近在咫尺，但转瞬间它又远离了你。真正的大庄家是美联储，小庄家是各种区块链社区（如以太坊），而矿工本质上只是被割韭菜的一群人。但是比特币成立的初衷，那种打破中心化、让货币自由流通的理念，我仍然认同。退出挖矿后，我把所有的以太坊换成了比特币，并决定长期持有，永不出售。

I first got involved in mining because of a deep learning course I was taking. It was the winter of 2020, and my own laptop was old and didn’t have a dedicated graphics card. The teacher recommended using Google’s Colab to train parameters, and for larger models, we used the HPC cluster at school. The HPC cluster was heavily utilized, especially during the days leading up to major assignment deadlines. I was completely unable to access it, but I envied a few classmates who had their own graphics card, the 3070, for training models.

However, when I searched online, I realized that the 3070 was simply unavailable. This was because Bitcoin and Ethereum were experiencing a major bull market, with prices increasing several times over. Miners were making a fortune (earning around 100 RMB per day with a graphics card, and the 3070’s original price was probably less than 3000 RMB). Consequently, the price of the 3070 skyrocketed to 10,000 RMB within a short period of time (yes, it tripled in price) … so I had no chance of buying a graphics card.

However, in March 2021, a glimmer of hope emerged. At that time, the only way to buy a graphics card was to purchase a whole computer. Brands like HP and Dell offered computers with the 3060Ti or 3070. The price of a complete computer was around $1699-1799. I decisively bought three complete computers and happily mined in an Airbnb room in Orlando, Florida for three months. I used NiceHash and never considered building a mining rig since I wasn’t familiar with computer assembly. I even thought about rushing to buy cards at Best Buy at the original price, but Americans would line up the day before at 5 p.m. with tents, and I couldn’t compete. By the time I arrived in the morning, all the spots were taken.

In September, when I went to New York, I realized that mining with complete computers was not profitable at all. I didn’t need the CPUs, memory, and motherboards. The best approach was to build my own mining rigs and try to grab cards at the original price. Micro Center in New York offered cards, but you had to keep an eye on them every day. So, I wrote a script and deployed it on AWS Lambda, using EventBridge to check at regular intervals. As soon as a card was available, I would receive an SNS notification on my phone. Thanks to this setup, I managed to grab 7 or 8 cards at the original price. I wasn’t picky and mainly focused on 3060Ti, 3070, 6700XT, and others. In addition to the cards I bought on eBay, by the end of 2021, I had a total of 28 GPUs in hand. I assembled three mining rigs and happily mined. The winter in New York was cold, but my home was warm.

At that time, mining had become very popular, and the Ethereum community started focusing on the 2.0 upgrade (Shanghai Forge) as a top roadmap item. Many people were concerned that mining would soon become unfeasible, so some started selling their mining cards. However, I wasn’t worried because I didn’t believe in the ETH 2.0 upgrade. In my opinion, the only truly decentralized consensus method was distributed hashing. The approach taken in the 2.0 version, with dozens of server centers, was essentially centralized and went against the original intention of blockchain. So, even if Ethereum couldn’t be mined, I believed there were other coins to mine.

The profits from mining were actually quite good for me. At its peak, I mined coins worth about $20,000. This covered the costs of many of the mining cards. As for electricity bills, I considered them as heating expenses, so I didn’t mind.

The real turning point came in the spring of 2022 when the Federal Reserve started tapering its balance sheet and interest rates soared. The cryptocurrency market was hit hard, and prices dropped by a third. I was deeply affected because I watched the value of my coins go from $20,000 to $6,000. It was something most people couldn’t accept. Many people started selling their cards, and electricity costs remained high, close to $1,000 per month. Continuing to mine would have been a loss-making business. Moreover, I was nearing graduation, so I packed up all my mining cards and came to Seattle to start a new life.

Looking back on my mining journey, it feels like a dream. You feel that wealth is within reach, but in an instant, it slips away from you. The real big players are the Federal Reserve, and the small players are various blockchain communities (like Ethereum). Miners, essentially, are just a group of people being exploited. However, I still believe in the original purpose of Bitcoin, the idea of breaking centralization and allowing free circulation of currency. After quitting mining, I converted all my Ethereum into Bitcoin, which I plan to hold for the long term and never sell.
